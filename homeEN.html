COD and COD eSPORTS History

      Historia do Call Of Duty

Call of Duty (or CoD) its an FPS game (First Pearson Shooter). It started out as a PC-only game that later expanded to various types of consoles.

The earliest versions of the game, specifically: Call of Duty, Call of Duty 2 and Call of Duty 3 were based on World War II. CoD began to build on

Modern Warfare from Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare, which was followed by Call of Duty: World at War and Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2. Black Ops which

are already based on the Cold War. From Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3, ie Call of Duty: Black Ops II, Call of Duty: Ghosts, Call of Duty: Advanced Warfare,

Call of Duty: Black Ops III, Call of Duty: Infinite Warfare were based on wartime in the near future. In 2017 Treyarch decides to bet once again on the

World War II theme and launches Call of Duty: WWII. In 2018 Treyarch decides to wager on Battle Royale games and launches Call of Duty: Black Ops IV.

Call of Duty: Black Ops IV was the first version of CoD that did not contain a Single Player mode (campaign mode) and focused solely on Multiplayer mode, Zombies and a

new type of battle royale called Blackout. In October 2019 Call of Duty: Modern Warfare was launched.

      Who created COD?

Call of Duty games are the property of Activision. While Infinity Ward studio is still the main producer, Treyarch has also produced

some versions where the story is interconnected between them.

      What are the eSPORTS?

The Call of Duty Championship is an annual tournament that is held at the end of the season to determine the World Champion of the Year. To qualify, teams

have to participate in events before the World Championship. Players to enter must be at least 18 years old.

      When did the eSPORTS start?

The beginning of this tournament was in 2013 with the Call of Duty: Black Ops II for the Xbox 360 and the winner was the team Fariko Impact.
